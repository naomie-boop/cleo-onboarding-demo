## Video 3 — The Signal Card (~5min)

[SCREEN: Dashboard]

We're going to open a signal to see everything Cleo gives you. I'll click on this one.

[ACTION: Click on a signal — the card opens]

OK, the signal card. This is where all the value of Cleo is concentrated. We'll go through it section by section.

[ACTION: Point to the header]

At the top, the header. You see the type badge — Sanction, Recommendation or Analysis. The relevant law, the country flag. And two important numbers: the relevance score — it's a percentage, the higher it is the more it directly concerns you — and the risk level, Critical, High, Medium or Low. In two seconds you know if it's urgent.

[ACTION: Scroll to "Summary for your role"]

First section: the summary for your role. And this is something really powerful. This text isn't the same for everyone. It's written based on your position. If you're a DPO, you get a data protection angle. If you're a product quality manager, it's a product compliance angle. If you're a lawyer, it's a legal angle. Same signal, same event, but the summary is adapted to what you need to know.

This depends on the Department and Role you've set in your Profile, in settings. If you change your role, the summaries change.

[ACTION: Scroll to "Impact for your role"]

Next, "Impact for your role". This is different from the summary. The summary explains what happened in the world. The impact connects it to your company. It tells you why this concerns you specifically, what are the risks for your business, what it concretely implies. Basically, it's the analysis that would normally take you two hours — reading the source, understanding the implications, cross-referencing with your context. Here, it's done for you.

[ACTION: Point to the right panel — "Products impacted"]

On the right, the impacted products. And note — these aren't generic categories. These are your products, the ones you added to your catalog. Cleo crossed the signal with your catalog and tells you precisely: these product lines are concerned. No guessing needed.

[ACTION: Scroll to "Obligations"]

Obligations. Cleo extracts the precise legal obligations from the signal. Each obligation cites its legal basis — the exact article, paragraph, reference to the law text. There's an assigned person for each obligation, and a checkbox to track your progress. It's your compliance checklist.

[ACTION: Scroll to "Action plan"]

The action plan — this is where it becomes truly actionable. Each action is concrete. You have an urgency indicator — red is immediate, orange is within the coming weeks. The action text, who's responsible, the timeline — "within 14 days", "within 1 month" — the legal reference, and which products are concerned.

It's structured, it's ready. You can copy-paste this into an email to your management or into a tracking ticket. No need to reword.

[ACTION: Scroll down]

Below, the regulatory context: the full history of the decision, with all references. This is what you put in your note to management to make your case.

Competitive exposure: Cleo tells you whether you're more or less exposed than your competitors in your sector. That helps you calibrate your response.

And the escalation line: who to escalate to in your organization.

[ACTION: Point to the bottom of the page]

At the very bottom, the official source — a direct link to the original document. You can verify for yourself in one click. And a feedback button, thumbs up thumbs down, to say whether it was relevant. The more you vote, the more Cleo refines its results for you.

There you go. In three minutes of reading, you have everything: what happened, why it concerns you, which products are affected, what to do, by when, on what legal basis, and who to escalate to. That's the signal card.

[ACTION: Point to the "Standard" column on the right]

And here, the important column: monitoring priority. By default everything is "Standard". But you can switch to "Enhanced" for the laws that matter most in your sector. Enhanced means Cleo scans them more frequently and surfaces the signals as a priority.

[ACTION: Click the "Standard" dropdown on a law and select "Enhanced"]

[ACTION: Point to the filters]

The filters: by risk — Critical, High, Moderate, Low. By status — In Force, Adopted, Proposed. And dropdowns by region, by compliance status, by product.

The "Adopted" filter, I'll say it again: these are laws that have been voted but aren't yet in force. That's your window to anticipate.

Here's what's coming that isn't yet enforced. This is where you can get ahead.

[ACTION: Point to the "+ Add a regulation" button]

[ACTION: Click "Authorities" in the menu]

Now the authorities. These are the bodies that enforce the laws. The CPSC in the US, the DGCCRF in France, ECHA for chemicals in Europe, the CMA in the UK.

457 authority groups. For each authority: the name, the number of regulations it manages, its country, the risk level, the number of impacts, and an activity bar.

## Video 7 — Settings and Sources (~4min)

[SCREEN: Dashboard]

Last video, the settings. This is important because it's where you configure what Cleo monitors and how it alerts you.

[ACTION: Click "Settings" in the menu to expand it]

Settings expands and we have several sub-sections. Let's start with the most important one.

[ACTION: Click "Profile"]

The profile — this is what personalizes everything in Cleo. Your department and your role — that's what determines the angle of summaries and impacts in the signal cards. If you're in the legal department with a DPO role, all summaries will be data protection-focused. If you're in product compliance, it's a quality and safety angle. So this is really the first thing to configure properly.

[ACTION: Click "Scans"]

Scans. This is where you manage your credits. Every time you click "Refresh" on the dashboard, it consumes credits. Cleo scans all sources, analyzes the results, crosses them with your products and markets. Here you see how many credits you have left, your consumption history.

On the Pro plan it's 5,000 credits per month. Purchased credits don't expire. You can see the padlock next to some options — certain features are reserved for the Pro plan.

[ACTION: Click "Sources" in the menu (below Settings)]

And now the sources. This is my favorite part because this is where you control where the information comes from.

[ACTION: Point to the title and subtitle]

"Quality of sources used by the monitoring pipeline." Basically, Cleo doesn't look for information just anywhere. It has a pipeline of official sources — government gazettes, federal registries, agency publications — and each source has a quality score.

[ACTION: Point to the "Discovered sources" list]

Here you see the list of automatically discovered sources. For each source: the domain — federalregister.gov, gov.uk, europarl.europa.eu — its quality score out of 10, the number of articles it provided, and its status.

[ACTION: Point to a source's score]

The score is a reliability rating. The higher it is, the more reliable the source. A federal government registry will always score higher than an opinion blog.

[ACTION: Point to the green "Authorized" badge]

The "Authorized" status in green means Cleo uses this source. And this is where you have control. You can block a source if it seems irrelevant or unreliable. You click it, switch it to "Blocked", and Cleo won't use it anymore in its analyses.

[ACTION: Point to the "Preferred sources" section at the top]

And here at the top, "Preferred sources". This is the reverse: you can add trusted sources.

[ACTION: Click in the search field]

You type a domain — for example, an official site you already follow — and click "Add". Cleo will prioritize it in every scan. That means articles from this source will be processed first in the analysis.

This is useful if you have specific industry sources that Cleo hasn't identified yet. You add them, they're prioritized, and the next scans will automatically include them.

[ACTION: Point to the menu on the left to show the other sub-sections]

To quickly wrap up the other settings: "Billing" to manage your plan — Free, Pro or Enterprise. "Members" to invite your team — on Pro you can have up to 5 members, each with their own profile and their own adapted summaries. "Integrations" to connect Slack if you want to receive alerts directly in a channel. And "Notifications" to configure alert types: critical alerts, weekly digest, deadline reminders.

And that's it for the full tour of Cleo Insight. In summary: you add your products and your markets, Cleo monitors 832 regulations and 457 authorities for you, and every morning your dashboard tells you exactly what to look at. It's built to save you time and make sure you don't miss anything.
