Getting Started

What is Cleo Comply?

Cleo Comply is an AI-powered regulatory intelligence platform. It monitors thousands of regulations across 106+ countries, cross-references them with your products and markets, and tells you exactly what to do — in plain language, adapted to your role.

📊

Real-time monitoring

Automated scans of 3,700+ official sources — federal registers, agency publications, official journals.

🎯

Product × regulation matching

Every signal is cross-referenced with your specific products and markets from your catalog.

👤

Role-based intelligence

Summaries and action plans adapt to your role — DPO, quality manager, legal counsel.

⚡

Actionable output

Legal obligations, action plans, deadlines, and responsible parties — ready to act on.

🚀 Quick Start

Cleo does the heavy lifting. You just enter your company domain — the AI handles the rest.

1

Sign up & enter your domain

Create your account at insight.cleolabs.co. Enter your company domain (e.g. electrolux.com). That's all Cleo needs to get started.

2

AI builds your regulatory profile

Cleo's AI automatically analyzes your domain and generates your entire regulatory landscape:

  • 🏭 Products — your product catalog, detected from public data
  • 🌍 Countries — your markets, identified from your footprint
  • ⚖️ Regulations — all applicable laws, cross-referenced
  • 🏛 Authorities — the regulatory bodies that matter to you
  • 📊 Signals — your first set of regulatory intelligence
⏳
This takes 15–30 minutes. Go grab a coffee — the AI is scanning thousands of sources, cross-referencing regulations, and building your personalized intelligence feed.
3

Review & validate

Once the scan completes, review what the AI generated. Go through each section and validate:

  • ✅ Products — are these your real products? Add missing ones, remove irrelevant ones
  • ✅ Countries — are these your real markets? Add or remove as needed
  • ✅ Regulations — correct? Adjust priority levels
  • ✅ Authorities — relevant to your sector?
💡
Think of it as proofreading AI's homework. The AI does 95% of the work. You just refine and confirm.
✓

You're set

Your dashboard is now live. Every day, Cleo monitors your regulatory landscape and surfaces new signals. Configure your scan frequency and notifications to stay ahead.

📊 Dashboard

The dashboard is your daily cockpit. It shows your regulatory landscape at a glance and surfaces what needs your attention.

The dashboard with KPI cards, filters, and signal feed

KPI Cards

Four key metrics at the top of the screen:

  • NOUVEAUX — number of new regulatory signals since your last visit, with a sparkline bar chart
  • EXPOSITION — your global risk level (Critique / Élevé / Modéré / Faible), with a colored dot indicator
  • ÉCHÉANCE — days until your next regulatory deadline (e.g. "36j"), with the regulation name below
  • RÉGLEMENTATIONS — total number of regulations being monitored for your organization

Filters

Two rows of quick filters let you slice your signal feed, plus dropdown filters for finer control:

  • IMPACT — Tous · Critique · Élevé · Modéré · Faible (pill buttons, selected = filled blue)
  • STATUT — Tous · En vigueur · Adopté · Proposé (pill buttons)
  • 4 dropdown filters — "Tous les produits" · "Tous les pays" · "Toutes les lois" · "Toutes les catégories" (Analyse / Sanction / Législation)
💡
Pro tip: Use the "Adopté" status filter to see laws that have been voted but aren't in force yet. This is your anticipation window — get ahead before enforcement begins.

Signal Feed

Below the filters, signals are grouped chronologically into three sections: HIER (yesterday), CETTE SEMAINE (this week), PLUS ANCIEN (older), each with a count badge. Each signal card shows:

  • A colored type badge — Législation / Sanction / Analyse
  • The law name and country flag
  • The age of the signal and a one-line title
  • Number of impacted products and risk level
  • A bookmark icon (flag outline) — click to save the signal to your Bookmarks page
  • A share icon (paper plane) — "Envoyer un signal" to share with a colleague

Actualiser

The Actualiser button (top-right, purple) triggers a new scan. Cleo scans all official sources, processes new documents, and updates your signals. Each scan consumes credits from your plan allocation.

📋 Signal Card

Click any signal to open its full card. This is where all the intelligence concentrates — the analysis that would normally take hours is done for you.

A signal card with AI summary, obligations, action plan, and competitive exposure

Header

The signal type badge (LÉGISLATION / SANCTION / ANALYSE), the source law name (e.g. BC PIPA, OFAC Sanctions), country flag, full title. Below: the relevance score as a percentage (e.g. 90%) and the risk level badge (Critical / High / Medium / Low).

AI Summary

An AI-generated summary personalized to your role. It starts with the key business impact for your organization (e.g. "creating direct and immediate exposure for Electrolux Professional AB given its subsidiaries in the UK and Russia"). A "Lire la suite ▼" button expands the full summary. The content adapts based on your Department and Role in Profile settings.

Conséquences Clés

A bullet list of the key consequences, each prefixed with a ⚡ icon. Immediate, concrete, and specific to your business context. A link like "+3 autres obligations ci-dessous" indicates there are additional obligations in the Obligations accordion below.

Produits Impactés

Product tags showing which items from your catalog are affected. Not generic categories — your actual products, cross-referenced automatically.

4 Accordion Sections

The signal card contains four expandable sections, each with its own icon:

  1. ⊕ VOTRE PLAN D'ACTION — Concrete actions with deadlines and responsible parties. Ready to assign to your team.
  2. 📋 OBLIGATIONS (count) — Legal obligations extracted from the regulation, with article references and checkboxes for compliance tracking. The count in parentheses shows the total (e.g. "OBLIGATIONS (6)").
  3. 📈 IMPACT POUR VOTRE RÔLE — Role-specific impact analysis that adapts based on the Department and Role set in your Profile.
  4. 📖 CONTEXTE RÉGLEMENTAIRE — Broader regulatory context: history, related regulations, enforcement trends.

Exposition Concurrentielle

Below the accordions, a fixed (always visible) section titled EXPOSITION CONCURRENTIELLE with a 📈 icon. It analyzes whether the regulatory exposure is symmetrical (same for all players in your industry) or asymmetrical (may advantage or disadvantage specific competitors based on their geography or supply chain).

ℹ️
The summary and role-impact section adapt based on the Department and Role configured in your Profile settings. Changing your role changes all summaries.

Source & Feedback

At the bottom of the signal card: a "Source: sec.gov" link (clickable, opens the original source document), plus a feedback prompt: "Cette source était-elle pertinente ?" with 👍 Oui / 👎 Non buttons. Your feedback improves future signal quality.

⚡ Scans

Configure automatic regulatory scans. Titled: Scans — Configurer les scans réglementaires automatiques.

Scan settings — frequency, preferred day, and automatic scan toggle

Scans automatiques

A toggle — "Scans automatiques — Rechercher automatiquement de nouveaux signaux réglementaires" — controls whether scans run on a schedule.

Fréquence des scans

When automatic scans are enabled, select one of five frequencies:

  • Quotidien — Tous les jours
  • Jours ouvrés — Lun – Ven
  • Hebdomadaire — Tous les 7 jours
  • Bimensuel — Tous les 14 jours
  • Mensuel — Tous les 30 jours

Jour préféré

A day-of-week selector (Dim / Lun / Mar / Mer / Jeu / Ven / Sam) — the preferred day for your scheduled scan to run.

Regulation-Specific Frequency

You can override the global frequency for individual regulations from the Regulations page — set specific regulations to weekly scanning or exclude them from automatic scans entirely.

ℹ️

How automatic scans work:

  • Scans check the last 30 days of regulatory news for all your monitored regulations.
  • You can modify the frequency per regulation on the Regulations page.
  • Regulations with compliance deadlines within 30 days are automatically scanned weekly, regardless of this setting.

Manual Scan

At any time, hit the Actualiser button on the Dashboard to trigger an on-demand scan. Each scan (automatic or manual) consumes credits.

❓ FAQ

How do automatic scans work?
Based on the frequency setting in Scans settings, Cleo automatically checks the last 30 days of regulatory news. You can override the frequency per individual regulation on the Regulations page. Regulations with deadlines within 30 days are scanned weekly regardless of your global setting.
What are credits?
Each scan (manual or automatic) consumes credits. The Pro plan includes 5,000 credits/month. Credits roll over — unused credits carry to the next month. You can buy additional credits anytime from the Billing page.
What's the difference between signal types?
  • Législation — a new law or regulation has been published or updated
  • Sanction — an enforcement action, fine, or penalty has been issued by an authority
  • Analyse — an analysis of the impact of an existing regulation on your sector or products
How does role-based personalization work?
Based on the Department and Role set in your Profile. When a regulatory event is processed, Cleo generates a summary tailored to your function. A DPO sees data protection angles; a Quality Manager sees safety angles; a Legal Counsel sees liability angles — same event, different perspective.
Can I share signals with my team?
Yes. Use the "Envoyer un signal" button on any signal card in the dashboard. It generates a shareable link or sends the signal directly to a colleague.
What does the AI badge mean?
The AI badge on a product, country, or authority means it was automatically discovered and enriched by Cleo's AI analysis — you didn't add it manually. You can always edit or delete AI-enriched entries.
Can I add regulations that aren't in the library?
Yes. Click "+ Ajouter une réglementation" on the Regulations page. Cleo will automatically enrich it with authority, jurisdiction, status, and related obligations.
What happens when I add a new country?
Cleo immediately starts monitoring local regulations for that market and cross-references them with your product catalog. New signals will appear on your next scan.
